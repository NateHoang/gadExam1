Chromosomal / Microdeletion
Syndrome Category Key features
Down syndrome (Trisomy 21) Chromosomal (Trisomy 21)
  • Intellectual disability
  • hypotonia
  • characteristic facies
  • cardiac defects
  • oral findings: Class III malocclusion
  • narrow palate
  • hypodontia
Patau syndrome (Trisomy 13) Chromosomal (Trisomy 13)
  • Severe CNS defects (holoprosencephaly)
  • polydactyly
  • microcephaly
  • often lethal
Cri‑du‑chat (5p deletion) Chromosomal deletion
  • High‑pitched cry
  • failure to thrive
  • hypotonia
  • intellectual delay
Turner syndrome Sex chromosome (45,X or mosaic)
  • Short stature
  • streak ovaries
  • lymphedema
  • neck webbing
  • congenital cardiac/renal defects
Klinefelter syndrome (XXY) Sex chromosome (extra X)
  • Hypogonadism
  • infertility
  • tall stature
  • feminized features
DiGeorge syndrome (22q11.2 deletion) Microdeletion (22q11.2)
  • Cleft palate
  • congenital heart disease
  • thymic hypoplasia → T‑cell dysfunction
  • hypocalcemia (parathyroid)
Prader‑Willi syndrome Imprinting disorder (paternal loss)
  • Infant hypotonia/poor feeding
  • childhood hyperphagia
  • obesity
  • hypogonadism
  • intellectual disability
Angelman syndrome Imprinting disorder (maternal loss)
  • Severe intellectual disability
  • seizures
  • ataxia
  • inappropriate laughter

Mendelian (single‑gene) — Autosomal dominant / recessive / X‑linked
Syndrome Category Key features
Apert syndrome Mendelian — Autosomal dominant (craniosynostosis)
  • Premature suture fusion
  • syndactyly
  • midface hypoplasia
  • dental crowding
  • possible cleft palate
Crouzon syndrome Mendelian — Autosomal dominant (craniosynostosis)
  • Midface hypoplasia
  • ocular proptosis
  • hypertelorism
  • variable skull shapes
Achondroplasia Mendelian — Autosomal dominant
  • FGFR3 mutation → disproportionate short stature (dwarfism)
Cleidocranial dysplasia Mendelian — Autosomal dominant
  • Clavicle hypoplasia/aplasia
  • open sutures
  • supernumerary teeth
  • delayed eruption
Cherubism Mendelian — Autosomal dominant
  • Bilateral jaw swelling
  • multilocular radiolucencies
  • dental displacement
EEC syndrome (Ectrodactyly‑Ectodermal Dysplasia‑Clefting) Mendelian — variable inheritance
  • Lobster‑claw deformities (ectrodactyly)
  • ectodermal defects (hair/nails/teeth)
  • cleft lip/palate
Ataxia‑Telangiectasia Mendelian — Autosomal recessive
  • ATM mutation → cerebellar ataxia
  • telangiectasias
  • immunodeficiency
  • cancer risk
Fragile X syndrome X‑linked (CGG repeat expansion)
  • Intellectual disability (males)
  • long narrow face
  • large ears
  • macroorchidism
Hunter syndrome (MPS II) X‑linked recessive (lysosomal storage)
  • Iduronate sulfatase deficiency → GAG buildup
  • coarsened facies
  • hepatosplenomegaly
  • airway/cardio issues
Hurler syndrome (MPS I) Autosomal recessive (lysosomal storage)
  • IDUA mutation → severe MPS
  • coarse facies
  • corneal clouding
  • organomegaly
  • short life expectancy
Papillon‑Lefèvre syndrome (PLS) Mendelian — Autosomal recessive
  • CTSC mutation → palmoplantar hyperkeratosis
  • early aggressive periodontitis
  • tooth loss
Gardner syndrome Mendelian — Autosomal dominant (APC gene)
  • Hundreds of colon polyps (cancer risk)
  • extra teeth
  • osteomas/desmoid tumors

Craniofacial / Neural crest (neurocristopathies) & Sequence
Syndrome / Sequence Category Key features
Pierre Robin sequence Sequence (mandibular micrognathia → glossoptosis)
  • Micrognathia → downward tongue
  • cleft palate
  • airway/feeding issues
Van der Woude syndrome Craniofacial (autosomal dominant)
  • Cleft lip/palate
  • paramedian lower lip pits
Treacher‑Collins syndrome Neurocristopathy — Autosomal dominant
  • Hypoplastic zygomas
  • mandibular hypoplasia
  • downward slanting palpebral fissures
  • hearing loss (conductive)
Holoprosencephaly (HPE) Developmental malformation / syndromic cause
  • Forebrain midline failure → facial dysmorphism (cyclopia to mild forms)
  • severe neuro impairment
DiGeorge (also neurocristopathy) (see above)
  • See DiGeorge entry

Connective tissue / skeletal disorders
Syndrome Category Key features
Ehlers‑Danlos syndrome Connective tissue disorder (various inheritance)
  • Hyperextensible skin
  • hypermobile joints
  • easy bruising
  • types IV & VIII → severe periodontal disease
  • aortic risk
Marfan syndrome Connective tissue — Autosomal dominant
  • Tall
  • arachnodactyly
  • aortic root dilation/aneurysm risk
  • high‑arched palate
  • TMJ issues
Osteogenesis imperfecta Collagen defect (various inheritance)
  • Fragile bones
  • blue sclerae
  • dentinogenesis imperfecta
  • hearing loss

Other / Vascular / Inflammatory / Non‑genetic
Syndrome Category Key features
Progressive hemifacial hypoplasia (Parry‑Romberg syndrome) Non‑genetic, sporadic
  • Unilateral facial atrophy
  • coup de sabre scleroderma
  • alopecia
  • delayed dental development
Kawasaki disease Pediatric vasculitis (likely infectious trigger)
  • Fever
  • rash
  • mucocutaneous changes
  • coronary artery inflammation → aneurysm risk
